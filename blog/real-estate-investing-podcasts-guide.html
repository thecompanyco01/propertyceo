The 15 Best Real Estate Investing Podcasts for 2026

March 9, 2026 · 18 min read · By PropertyCEO

Whether you're buying your first rental property or scaling a portfolio of 50+ units, the right real estate investing podcast can compress years of learning into your daily commute. Podcasts give you direct access to investors who've already made the mistakes you're about to make — and they share those lessons for free.

But with hundreds of real estate shows out there, most investors waste time on the wrong ones. Some podcasts are thinly veiled sales pitches. Others rehash the same generic advice. And a few are genuinely transformative — the kind that change how you analyze deals, structure financing, and manage properties.

We've listened to and evaluated dozens of shows to bring you the 15 best real estate investing podcasts worth your time in 2026. For each one, we'll cover what it teaches, how often new episodes drop, and who will get the most value from it.

Why Real Estate Investing Podcasts Matter

Before we dive into the list, let's talk about why podcasts are such a powerful tool for real estate investors — especially those getting into property management.

Free, high-quality education. A single podcast episode can teach you more about cap rates, deal analysis, or tenant screening than a $500 weekend seminar. The best hosts bring on guests who've closed hundreds of deals and are willing to share exact numbers.

Passive learning on your schedule. You can absorb investing knowledge while driving, exercising, or doing property walk-throughs. Most investors we know consume 5-10 hours of podcast content per week without setting aside dedicated study time.

Market pulse. Real estate markets shift constantly. Podcasts that release weekly episodes keep you updated on interest rate changes, emerging markets, and shifts in rental rates by market in near-real-time.

Network by proxy. Even if you can't attend every real estate meetup, podcasts let you learn from the same people presenting at national conferences. Many investors credit a podcast episode with introducing them to a strategy that shaped their entire portfolio.

The 15 Best Real Estate Investing Podcasts

1. BiggerPockets Real Estate Podcast

Weekly 60-90 min episodes Best for: All levels

The undisputed king of the real estate investing podcast world. BiggerPockets has been the gateway show for hundreds of thousands of investors since 2013. Hosts David Greene and Rob Abasolo interview investors from every niche — single-family rentals, multifamily syndication, short-term rentals, commercial, and more.

What makes it essential: The guests share real numbers. You'll hear exact purchase prices, renovation costs, rent rolls, and returns. The community behind BiggerPockets (forums, calculators, tools) makes it more than just a podcast — it's an ecosystem. If you only listen to one real estate investing podcast, make it this one.

Standout episodes to start with: Their "rookie" series episodes for beginners and any episode featuring Brandon Turner's breakdown of the BRRRR method.

2. The Real Estate Guys Radio Show

Weekly 45-60 min episodes Best for: Intermediate to advanced

Robert Helms and Russell Gray have been broadcasting since 1997, making this one of the longest-running real estate investing podcasts in existence. They bring a macro-economic perspective that most shows lack — expect deep dives into interest rates, monetary policy, market cycles, and how global events impact real estate.

What makes it essential: Where other podcasts focus on tactics, The Real Estate Guys help you think strategically. Their annual Investor Summit at Sea connects listeners with industry experts and syndicators. If you're investing in multifamily or apartment buildings, their syndication-focused episodes are gold.

3. Afford Anything with Paula Pant

Weekly 45-75 min episodes Best for: Beginners and FIRE-minded investors

Paula Pant built a rental property portfolio that generates enough passive income to fund her lifestyle — and she did it by being ruthlessly strategic. Her podcast blends real estate investing with broader personal finance, decision-making, and financial independence principles.

What makes it essential: Paula's mantra — "You can afford anything but not everything" — forces you to think about opportunity cost. She covers rental property economics honestly, including the parts most hosts skip: tenant headaches, maintenance surprises, and the real time commitment of self-managing properties. Her interviewing style pulls genuinely useful insights from guests.

4. Best Real Estate Investing Advice Ever (Joe Fairless)

Daily 15-25 min episodes Best for: Syndication and commercial investors

Joe Fairless built a $2.7 billion real estate portfolio and his daily podcast is the most prolific in the industry — over 3,500 episodes and counting. Each episode features a different investor sharing their single best piece of advice, making it perfect for bite-sized learning.

What makes it essential: The daily format means you get exposed to a massive variety of strategies, markets, and deal types. Joe's focus on apartment syndication and value-add multifamily investing makes this essential for anyone thinking beyond single-family homes. The short episodes are perfect for a quick commute listen.

5. Rental Income Podcast with Dan Lane

Weekly 25-40 min episodes Best for: Buy-and-hold landlords

If you're focused specifically on building a portfolio of rental properties, this is your show. Dan Lane interviews everyday landlords — not Instagram influencers or syndication gurus — about how they built rental income streams. Guests share exact numbers: purchase prices, rents, cash flow, and expenses.

What makes it essential: The focus on regular people achieving financial freedom through rentals is refreshing and relatable. You'll learn practical tactics for tenant screening, rent optimization, and property management that you can implement immediately. Great complement to more strategy-heavy shows.

6. The Real Wealth Show with Kathy Fettke

Weekly 30-45 min episodes Best for: Passive and out-of-state investors

Kathy Fettke is one of the most respected voices in real estate investing, and her podcast focuses heavily on market analysis and turnkey investing. If you're investing remotely in the best cities for rental property, her data-driven market breakdowns are invaluable.

What makes it essential: Kathy and her guests identify emerging markets before they get crowded. The show regularly features economists, market analysts, and experienced investors who share proprietary research on population growth, job creation, and rent trends in specific metros.

7. BiggerPockets Rookie Podcast

Twice weekly 45-60 min episodes Best for: Complete beginners

A spin-off from the main BiggerPockets show, the Rookie Podcast is specifically designed for investors with zero to a few deals under their belt. Hosts Ashley Kehr and Tony Robinson walk through deals step-by-step, break down financing options, and answer listener questions that more advanced shows skip over.

What makes it essential: No question is too basic here. If you're still figuring out the difference between a cap rate and cash-on-cash return, or you're not sure how to find your first deal, this podcast meets you where you are. The hosts are encouraging without being unrealistic about the challenges.

8. The Property Management Show (Fourandhalf)

Weekly 20-35 min episodes Best for: Property managers and PM business owners

This is the premier podcast specifically for the property management industry. It covers marketing for PM companies, operational efficiency, technology adoption, team building, and the business side of managing other people's properties. If you're running or starting a property management business, this is required listening.

What makes it essential: Unlike investor-focused podcasts, this show addresses the unique challenges of property management as a business — from choosing the right CRM to handling owner expectations, scaling your portfolio, and pricing your services competitively.

9. Investing in Real Estate with Clayton Morris

Weekly 30-45 min episodes Best for: Turnkey and passive investors

Clayton Morris, former Fox News anchor turned real estate investor, focuses on building passive income through rental properties. His approach emphasizes buying affordable, cash-flowing properties in secondary markets and building a portfolio systematically.

What makes it essential: The show is action-oriented and concise. Morris shares his exact investing criteria and numbers from his own portfolio. His focus on financial freedom through real estate (rather than speculation or flipping) aligns well with long-term wealth building. Good episodes on understanding rental property depreciation and tax strategies.

10. The Real Estate InvestHER Podcast

Weekly 30-50 min episodes Best for: Women investors and anyone seeking diverse perspectives

Liz Faircloth and Andresa Guidelli created InvestHER to highlight women investors who are building impressive portfolios and businesses. The podcast covers everything from house hacking to commercial investing, with guests sharing real deal breakdowns and lessons learned.

What makes it essential: Beyond the important mission of amplifying underrepresented voices, this is simply a well-produced investing podcast with high-caliber guests. The interviews go deep on mindset, partnerships, and creative financing strategies that are useful regardless of your background.

11. The Old Capital Real Estate Investing Podcast

Weekly 25-40 min episodes Best for: Multifamily and commercial investors

Michael Becker and Paul Peebles specialize in multifamily lending and investing. Their podcast dives deep into commercial real estate financing, deal structuring, and the apartment investing lifecycle. If you're moving beyond single-family into apartment buildings, this show fills critical knowledge gaps.

What makes it essential: The hosts are active in the debt side of multifamily, giving you an insider's perspective on what lenders look for, how to structure deals that actually get funded, and common mistakes that kill apartment deals. Understanding financing is often the bottleneck for scaling investors.

12. Coach Carson Real Estate Podcast

Weekly 30-50 min episodes Best for: Small portfolio builders focused on financial independence

Chad "Coach" Carson built a portfolio of rental properties that funds his family's lifestyle, including extended international travel. His podcast focuses on the intersection of real estate investing and designing your ideal life. It's practical, grounded, and refreshingly free of hype.

What makes it essential: Coach Carson proves you don't need 500 units to achieve financial freedom. His episodes on house hacking, small multifamily investing, and using 1031 exchanges to grow tax-efficiently are among the best available. He's also transparent about mistakes, which makes the learning more authentic.

13. The Ken McElroy Real Estate Strategies Podcast

Weekly 20-40 min episodes Best for: Aspiring and active apartment investors

Ken McElroy manages over $1 billion in real estate assets and was Robert Kiyosaki's Rich Dad Advisor for real estate. His podcast draws from decades of hands-on experience in acquiring, managing, and selling large apartment complexes.

What makes it essential: Ken's property management perspective is rare among real estate investing podcasts. He talks about the operational realities of managing hundreds of units — maintenance, staffing, tenant retention, and value-add renovations. Essential listening if you're scaling into professional-level property management.

14. SparkRental's Financial Freedom with Real Estate Investing

Weekly 30-45 min episodes Best for: Landlords and co-investing enthusiasts

Brian Davis and Deni Supplee cover the practical side of landlording: tenant management, rent collection, legal issues, and building passive income streams. The show also explores co-investing and real estate investment clubs as ways to scale without taking on all the risk yourself.

What makes it essential: The focus on the nuts and bolts of being a landlord makes this a great complement to the more strategy-focused shows on this list. Episodes on legal notices, lease structures, and accounting for landlords solve immediate problems.

15. Lifetime Cash Flow Through Real Estate (Rod Khleif)

Twice weekly 30-60 min episodes Best for: Multifamily investors and mindset-driven achievers

Rod Khleif lost $50 million in the 2008 crash and rebuilt from scratch. His podcast combines hard-nosed multifamily investing education with motivational content. Rod has built one of the largest multifamily investing communities in the country through his bootcamps and mentoring programs.

What makes it essential: Rod's personal story of catastrophic loss and comeback gives him unique credibility on risk management — something most investing podcasts gloss over. His episodes on analyzing multifamily deals, raising capital, and avoiding common pitfalls are battle-tested. The mindset episodes aren't for everyone, but the investing content is top-tier.

How to Use Real Estate Investing Podcasts for Property Management Education

If you're specifically interested in building a property management business or becoming a better self-managing landlord, podcasts can be a structured educational tool — not just background noise. Here's how to get the most out of them:

Create a Curriculum, Not a Playlist

Don't just subscribe to every show and listen randomly. Instead, identify the specific skills you need to develop and find episodes that address them. For example:

  • Tenant screening: BiggerPockets episodes #312, #445 + Rental Income Podcast's screening series
  • Maintenance systems: The Property Management Show's operations episodes + Ken McElroy on vendor management
  • Legal compliance: SparkRental's episodes on property law and eviction procedures
  • Financial analysis: Coach Carson on cap rate calculations + Afford Anything on deal analysis
  • Scaling operations: Joe Fairless on property management at scale + The Property Management Show on growth

Take Notes and Implement

The biggest mistake podcast listeners make is treating content as entertainment rather than education. Keep a simple note system:

  1. Capture: Write down one actionable idea per episode
  2. Evaluate: Does this apply to your current situation?
  3. Implement: Set a deadline to test the idea on your next deal or property
  4. Review: Did it work? What would you change?

Investors who take this approach consistently report that a single podcast insight — a new screening question, a lease clause, a maintenance workflow — has saved them thousands of dollars or hours of headaches.

Listen for Property Management Systems

Many episodes, even on investor-focused shows, contain property management gems. Listen specifically for:

  • Tenant retention strategies — reducing turnover is the highest-ROI activity in property management
  • Rent optimization approaches — how top investors price their rentals for maximum occupancy and revenue
  • Vendor management systems — how investors managing 20+ units handle maintenance efficiently
  • Technology recommendations — which rent collection apps and management platforms real investors actually use
  • Legal lessons learned — cautionary tales about evictions, fair housing, and liability that protect your business

Join Podcast Communities

Most top podcasts have associated communities — Facebook groups, forums, or Discord servers — where listeners discuss episodes and share deals. These communities are where passive listening turns into active networking. The BiggerPockets forums alone have connected thousands of investors with partners, lenders, and mentors.

Pro tip: Listen to the same episode twice. First for the big ideas, second for the specific tactics and numbers. You'll catch details you missed the first time — especially deal breakdowns where hosts share exact cap rates, renovation budgets, and cash flow numbers.

How to Choose the Right Real Estate Investing Podcast for Your Stage

Not every podcast is right for every investor. Here's a quick guide based on where you are in your journey:

Complete Beginner (0 deals)

Start with: BiggerPockets Rookie, Afford Anything, and Coach Carson. These shows explain fundamentals without assuming you already know the jargon. Focus on understanding how deals work before diving into advanced strategies.

Early Investor (1-5 properties)

Add: Rental Income Podcast, SparkRental, and the main BiggerPockets show. You need tactical property management knowledge and exposure to different strategies. Start thinking about whether you want to self-manage or hire a property management company.

Scaling Investor (5-20+ units)

Focus on: The Real Estate Guys, Joe Fairless, Old Capital, and Ken McElroy. At this stage, you're thinking about commercial financing, syndication, property management systems, and tax strategies like bonus depreciation and 1031 exchanges.

PM Business Owner

Essential: The Property Management Show plus selected episodes from investor-focused podcasts. Understanding what investors want from their property managers makes you better at acquiring and retaining owner clients. Combine with our property management business guide.

Making the Most of Your Podcast Time

Here are practical habits that separate investors who learn from podcasts from those who just consume them:

Speed up playback. Most podcast apps let you listen at 1.5x or 2x speed. Once you're used to it, you'll never go back. This effectively doubles the amount of education you can absorb in the same time.

Create a "to-implement" list. Every week, pick one idea from the podcasts you listened to and commit to implementing it. A new tenant screening question. A rent analysis on one of your properties. A call to a lender about commercial financing. Small actions compound.

Share what you learn. Teaching others what you learn on podcasts — whether in a local REI group, online forum, or even just texting a friend — dramatically increases retention. The best investors are also the best teachers.

Don't forget to act. The biggest trap with podcasts is perpetual learning syndrome. At some point, you have enough knowledge to pick a market, analyze a deal, and make an offer. No amount of podcast episodes replaces the education of closing your first deal.

Final Thoughts

The right real estate investing podcast can be the difference between a stalled investing career and one that compounds year after year. The 15 shows listed here represent the best education available for free in the real estate investing space. They cover every stage from first deal anxiety to managing a multimillion-dollar portfolio.

Start with 2-3 shows that match your current level, listen consistently, take notes, and — most importantly — implement what you learn. The most successful real estate investors we know all credit podcasts with accelerating their education, but none of them got rich just by listening. Knowledge without action is just entertainment.

If you're serious about building wealth through real estate — whether as an active investor or through property management as a business — these 15 podcasts are your free MBA. Start listening today.
