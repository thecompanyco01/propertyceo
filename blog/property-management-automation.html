Property Management Automation: 20 Tasks You Should Stop Doing Manually

March 6, 2026 · 13 min read · By PropertyCEO

If you're managing more than 50 doors and still manually sending rent reminders, typing up maintenance work orders, or copying data between spreadsheets — you're burning 15-20 hours a week on work a computer should be doing.

Here are 20 tasks to automate, organized by category, with the tools to do it.

Tenant Communication (Save 4-6 hrs/week)

1

Rent Payment Reminders 2 hrs/week

Auto-send reminders on the 1st, 3rd, and 5th of each month. Include payment link. Escalate tone each time.

Tools: AppFolio, Buildium, or Zapier + your PM software
2

Lease Expiration Notices 1 hr/week

90/60/30-day automated emails to tenants with lease renewal options and rent adjustment. No more tracking expiration dates manually.

Tools: PM software auto-workflows or Calendly + email automation
3

Move-In/Move-Out Instructions 30 min/week

Trigger automated email sequences when lease status changes. Include utility setup guides, inspection checklists, key return instructions.

Tools: Email sequences in your PM software
4

Maintenance Status Updates 1 hr/week

Auto-text tenants when a work order is received, assigned, scheduled, and completed. They stop calling you asking "what's the status?"

Tools: Property Meld, Latchel, or built-in PM software notifications

Owner Communication (Save 3-5 hrs/week)

5

Monthly Owner Statements 2 hrs/week

Auto-generate and email owner statements by the 10th of each month. Include income, expenses, maintenance summary. Your PM software does this — turn it on.

Tools: AppFolio, Buildium, Rent Manager — all have auto-statements
6

Vacancy Notifications 30 min/week

Auto-notify owners when a notice-to-vacate is received, including your plan for re-leasing and timeline expectations.

Tools: Email template triggered by status change
7

Annual Tax Document Distribution 4 hrs/year

Auto-generate and send 1099s and year-end statements in January. Set it up once, never think about it again.

Tools: Your PM software + accounting integration

Leasing (Save 3-4 hrs/week)

8

Listing Syndication 1 hr/week

List a vacancy once, auto-publish to Zillow, Apartments.com, Facebook Marketplace, Realtor.com. No more logging into 6 platforms.

Tools: Buildium, AppFolio, or Tenant Turner
9

Showing Scheduling 2 hrs/week

Let prospects self-schedule showings online. Auto-confirm, auto-remind, auto-follow-up if they no-show. Stop playing phone tag.

Tools: Tenant Turner, Showdigs, Rently
10

Application Processing 1 hr/week

Online applications with auto-screening: credit check, criminal background, eviction history, income verification. Get a approve/deny recommendation in minutes.

Tools: TransUnion SmartMove, RentPrep, built-in PM software screening

Maintenance (Save 3-4 hrs/week)

11

Work Order Triage 1 hr/week

Auto-categorize incoming maintenance requests by priority. Emergency (water leak, no heat) → immediate dispatch. Routine → queue for next business day.

Tools: Property Meld, Latchel (AI triage)
12

Vendor Dispatch 1 hr/week

Auto-assign vendors based on work order category. Plumbing → your plumber. HVAC → your HVAC guy. Include property access instructions automatically.

Tools: Property Meld, or Zapier connecting your PM software to vendor SMS
13

Preventive Maintenance Scheduling 30 min/week

Auto-schedule HVAC filter changes, gutter cleaning, pest control on recurring calendars. Prevents expensive emergency repairs.

Tools: Google Calendar automations, PM software recurring tasks

Accounting (Save 2-3 hrs/week)

14

Rent Collection & Late Fees 1 hr/week

Auto-charge late fees on the 6th. Auto-send late notices. Auto-post payments from tenant portals. Zero manual entry.

Tools: Your PM software — this should already be on
15

Owner Payments/Distributions 1 hr/week

Auto-calculate and ACH owner distributions on the 10th-15th. Include statement. No manual checks, no trips to the bank.

Tools: PM software ACH integration
16

Invoice Processing 30 min/week

Vendors email invoices → auto-extract amount, property, category → create bill in your PM software. No more manual data entry from PDFs.

Tools: DEXT (formerly Receipt Bank), Zapier + AI extraction

Marketing & Growth (Save 2-3 hrs/week)

17

Review Requests 30 min/week

Auto-send a review request to every new owner at the 60-day mark and every tenant at move-in + 6 months. Include direct Google Review link.

Tools: Birdeye, Podium, or Mailchimp automation
18

Lead Follow-Up 1 hr/week

Owner lead comes in → auto-send intro email within 5 minutes → auto-schedule follow-up call for next business day → auto-send drip emails for 30 days if they don't sign.

Tools: CRM (HubSpot free tier, FollowUpBoss) + email sequences
19

Social Media Posting 1 hr/week

Batch-create 30 days of posts. Schedule them. Auto-share new listings to Facebook and Instagram. Stop posting in real-time.

Tools: Buffer, Hootsuite, or Meta Business Suite (free)
20

Market Rent Analysis 30 min/week

Auto-pull comp data for your portfolio monthly. Flag units renting 10%+ below market. Send owner a rent increase recommendation with data.

Tools: Rentometer API, Zillow Rental Manager, or manual Zillow + Apartments.com comps

💡 Total time saved by automating all 20: 15-20 hours per week. That's a full-time employee's worth of labor. At $25/hr, that's $32,500/year in recovered capacity — without hiring.

Where to Start

Don't try to automate everything at once. Here's the priority order:

  1. Week 1: Turn on automated rent reminders and late fees (items 1, 14)
  2. Week 2: Set up auto-owner statements (item 5)
  3. Week 3: Enable listing syndication and self-scheduled showings (items 8, 9)
  4. Week 4: Implement maintenance status notifications (item 4)
  5. Month 2: Build your lead follow-up sequence (item 18)
  6. Month 3: Everything else
