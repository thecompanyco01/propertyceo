Best Landlord Software in 2026: Top 10 Options Compared

Updated March 2026 · 18 min read

The right landlord software can save you 10–15 hours per month on rent collection, maintenance tracking, tenant communication, and accounting. The wrong one creates more problems than it solves. With dozens of options on the market — from free tools for single-unit landlords to enterprise platforms managing thousands of doors — choosing the best landlord software for your portfolio size and needs is critical.

We've tested, researched, and compared the top 10 landlord software platforms in 2026. This guide covers features, pricing, pros, cons, and which platform works best for different portfolio sizes — whether you own 1 unit or 500+.

What to Look for in Landlord Software

Before diving into specific platforms, here are the core features every landlord software should include:

  • Online rent collection: ACH payments, credit card options, automatic late fees, and payment tracking
  • Tenant screening: Credit checks, background checks, eviction history, and income verification
  • Maintenance tracking: Tenant request portals, work order management, vendor coordination
  • Lease management: Digital lease creation, e-signatures, renewal reminders, document storage
  • Accounting & reporting: Income/expense tracking, tax-ready reports, bank reconciliation
  • Tenant communication: In-app messaging, announcements, document sharing
  • Mobile app: Both landlord and tenant-facing mobile applications

Detailed Reviews: The Best Landlord Software in 2026

FREE TIER

1. Avail (by Realtor.com) — Best Free Landlord Software for Beginners

Avail is purpose-built for independent landlords with small portfolios. The free tier includes listing syndication, tenant screening (tenant pays), online rent collection via ACH, maintenance tracking, and state-specific lease templates. The premium Unlimited Plus plan ($7/unit/month) adds next-day rent payments, waived ACH fees, and custom lease clauses.

Pros: Genuinely useful free tier, excellent lease builder with state-specific clauses, intuitive interface, strong listing syndication to Realtor.com and partner sites.

Cons: Limited reporting and accounting features, ACH fees on free plan ($2.50/transaction charged to tenant), no mobile app for landlords (web only), limited scalability beyond 25 units.

Best for: First-time landlords with 1–10 units who want a simple, free starting point.

FREE TIER

2. TurboTenant — Best for Cost-Conscious Landlords

TurboTenant offers the most generous free plan in the market. Free features include unlimited property listings, tenant screening (tenant pays), rent collection, maintenance requests, lease agreements, and basic accounting. The Premium plan ($8.25/month billed annually) adds expedited rent payments, landlord-paid screening, and premium lease features.

Pros: Unlimited properties on free plan, excellent listing distribution (to Zillow, Apartments.com, etc.), tenant screening included, growing feature set with regular updates.

Cons: Accounting features are basic compared to Stessa, ACH rent collection takes 3–5 business days on free plan, limited customization for larger portfolios.

Best for: Landlords with 1–25 units who want comprehensive features without paying monthly fees.

FREE TIER

3. Stessa — Best for Financial Tracking and Tax Preparation

Stessa stands out for its financial tracking capabilities. It automatically imports transactions from linked bank accounts and credit cards, categorizes expenses, tracks income by property, and generates tax-ready financial reports including Schedule E. The free plan covers unlimited properties with full financial tracking. The Pro plan ($20/month) adds market rent estimates, unlimited document storage, and priority support.

Pros: Exceptional financial tracking and reporting, automatic transaction importing, tax-ready reports, clean dashboard showing portfolio performance at a glance.

Cons: Rent collection and tenant management are secondary features, tenant screening through third-party integration only, less robust maintenance tracking than competitors.

Best for: Investors with 5–50 units who prioritize financial tracking, performance analysis, and tax preparation.

FREE TIER

4. Baselane — Best Banking + Property Management Combo

Baselane combines property management with built-in banking. You get a dedicated landlord bank account with up to 4.19% APY on deposits, virtual sub-accounts for each property, automated rent collection, bookkeeping, and reporting — all free. Revenue comes from the banking relationship, so the PM features are genuinely free with no catches.

Pros: Truly free with no premium tier needed, high-yield banking for landlord funds, automatic bookkeeping linked to bank transactions, clean modern interface.

Cons: Newer platform with smaller user base, maintenance tracking is basic, limited third-party integrations, requires using Baselane banking (not for everyone).

Best for: Landlords with 1–30 units who want an all-in-one banking and property management solution with no monthly fees.

PAID

5. RentRedi — Best All-in-One for Growing Landlords

RentRedi packs an impressive feature set into an affordable package. For $12/month (billed annually), you get unlimited units, tenant screening, online rent collection, maintenance tracking with video uploads, lease management, listing syndication, and a tenant mobile app. Prequalification features let tenants submit applications before showings, saving you time.

Pros: Unlimited units at a flat rate (huge value for growing portfolios), excellent mobile app for both landlords and tenants, video maintenance requests, prequalification screening, REI Hub accounting integration.

Cons: No free tier (though pricing is very competitive), accounting requires REI Hub add-on ($15/month), interface can feel cluttered with all features packed in.

Best for: Landlords with 10–100 units who want comprehensive features at a predictable flat rate.

PROFESSIONAL

6. Buildium — Best for Growing Property Management Companies

Buildium is the bridge between landlord software and professional property management platforms. It handles everything from rent collection and maintenance to full trust accounting, owner statements, CAM charges, and association management. Starting at $58/month for up to 150 units, it's designed for property managers who are scaling beyond a handful of properties.

Pros: Full trust accounting and owner statements, association management features, built-in tenant screening, robust reporting, handles mixed portfolios (residential, commercial, associations).

Cons: Steep price jump from landlord-tier software, learning curve for new users, some features feel dated compared to newer platforms, per-unit pricing adds up.

Best for: Property managers with 50–500 units who need professional-grade accounting and owner reporting.

PROFESSIONAL

7. AppFolio — Best for Professional Property Managers (200+ Units)

AppFolio is the industry standard for professional property management companies. It offers AI-powered leasing assistants, online portals for tenants, owners, and vendors, full accounting, maintenance workflows, and a mobile app. Minimum portfolio requirement of 200 units reflects its positioning for serious operators.

Pros: Most feature-complete platform available, AI tools for leasing and maintenance, excellent owner portal, strong integrations, continually innovating.

Cons: 200-unit minimum and $1.40/unit/month minimum pricing, implementation can take weeks, overkill for small portfolios, onboarding fee required.

Best for: Property management companies with 200+ units that need enterprise-grade features and are willing to invest in the platform.

8. Rent Manager — Best for Customization and Complex Portfolios

Rent Manager is highly customizable with robust reporting, workflows, and integrations. It handles residential, commercial, and mixed-use properties with detailed unit-level tracking. Custom pricing based on portfolio size and features needed.

Pros: Extremely customizable workflows, handles complex mixed-use portfolios, strong reporting engine, built-in website builder, good API for integrations.

Cons: Steeper learning curve than competitors, interface is functional but dated, custom pricing means you need to negotiate, better suited for experienced operators.

Best for: Experienced property managers with 200+ mixed-portfolio units who need deep customization.

9. Propertyware — Best for Single-Family Rental Portfolios

Propertyware (by RealPage) is specifically designed for single-family rental management at scale. It includes drive-time-based maintenance scheduling, field service management, and portfolio analytics tailored to scattered-site management — challenges unique to SFR portfolios.

Pros: Purpose-built for SFR management, excellent field service and maintenance features, strong reporting, good mobile app for field staff.

10. DoorLoop — Best Modern Interface for Mid-Size Portfolios

DoorLoop launched as a modern alternative to legacy platforms like Buildium and AppFolio. It features a clean, intuitive interface with full property management capabilities: accounting, CRM, tenant portal, maintenance, and reporting. Starting at $59/month for up to 20 units.

Pros: Modern, intuitive UI (fastest to learn), QuickBooks Online integration, good mobile experience, responsive customer support, CRM features for lead tracking.

Cons: Per-unit pricing gets expensive at scale, newer platform (less battle-tested than Buildium/AppFolio), some advanced features still in development.

Best for: Property managers with 50–500 units who want a modern, easy-to-use platform and are willing to trade some advanced features for better UX.

Which Landlord Software Should You Choose?

The best landlord software depends entirely on your portfolio size and what you need most:

1–10 Units: Start Free

Use Avail or TurboTenant for basic property management, or Stessa if financial tracking is your priority. All three are free for small portfolios and cover the essentials. Add Baselane if you want integrated banking.

10–50 Units: Invest in Efficiency

RentRedi offers the best value at this scale — unlimited units for a flat $12/month (annual billing). If accounting is your main pain point, combine Stessa (free) with a separate rent collection tool.

50–200 Units: Go Professional

Buildium or DoorLoop provide the accounting, reporting, and owner management features you need to operate professionally. Buildium is more established; DoorLoop has a better interface.

200+ Units: Enterprise Grade

AppFolio for well-rounded professional management, Rent Manager for complex/customizable needs, or Propertyware for single-family rental portfolios at scale.

Pro Tip: Don't over-buy software. A landlord with 5 units doesn't need AppFolio any more than a teenager needs a Ferrari. Start with a free or low-cost platform, learn what features actually matter to your operations, and upgrade as your portfolio grows. You can always migrate later — most platforms offer import tools.

Key Features Deep Dive

Online Rent Collection

Every modern landlord software platform offers online rent collection, but implementation varies. Key differences to evaluate:

  • Payment speed: Free platforms typically process ACH in 3–5 business days. Paid platforms offer 1–2 day processing.
  • Payment methods: ACH (bank transfer), credit/debit card, and sometimes cash payment options through retail partners
  • Automatic payments: Tenants can set up autopay — this alone improves on-time payment rates by 20–30%
  • Late fee automation: The software calculates and applies late fees based on your lease terms. No awkward conversations required.

Tenant Screening

Most platforms either include screening or integrate with TransUnion, Experian, or similar providers. Compare:

  • Who pays: Landlord-paid screening ($25–$40/applicant) vs. tenant-paid (free to you, but may reduce applications)
  • What's included: Credit report, criminal background, eviction history, income verification. The best platforms include all four.
  • Speed: Most results are instant or within minutes

Maintenance Management

Efficient maintenance tracking separates good landlord software from great:

  • Tenant portal: Tenants submit requests online with photos/videos — no more phone calls at midnight
  • Work order tracking: Assign to vendors, track status, document completion
  • Vendor management: Store vendor contacts, track spending, manage recurring maintenance
  • Communication log: Every interaction documented — crucial for legal protection

Switching Software: Migration Tips

If you're currently using spreadsheets or switching between platforms, plan your migration carefully:

  1. Export all data first: Download tenant information, lease documents, transaction history, and maintenance records from your current system
  2. Time the switch between months: Start the new platform at the beginning of a month when there are no pending transactions
  3. Notify tenants early: Give 30 days notice about the new payment portal. Provide setup instructions and support.
  4. Run parallel for one month: Keep the old system active while the new one is being set up to ensure no data is lost
  5. Verify everything: Check that all leases, balances, and tenant information transferred correctly before decommissioning the old platform

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the best free landlord software?

For overall property management, TurboTenant offers the most complete free package (unlimited properties, screening, rent collection, maintenance). For financial tracking specifically, Stessa is unmatched. For integrated banking, Baselane is the best option.

Is landlord software worth paying for?

If you have more than 10 units, yes. The time saved on rent collection, maintenance coordination, and accounting easily justifies $12–$60/month. For 1–10 units, free options are usually sufficient.

Can I use landlord software for commercial properties?

Most platforms on this list focus on residential. For commercial property management, look at Buildium (handles mixed portfolios), Rent Manager (strong commercial features), or dedicated commercial platforms like Yardi Breeze or MRI Software.

Do tenants have to use the software too?

Tenants use the tenant-facing portal to pay rent, submit maintenance requests, and access lease documents. Most platforms offer tenant mobile apps that make the experience seamless. Tenants generally appreciate the convenience of online payments and digital maintenance requests.

Final Thoughts

The best landlord software is the one you'll actually use consistently. Don't get paralyzed by feature comparisons — pick a platform that matches your current portfolio size, start using it this week, and upgrade when you outgrow it. The biggest efficiency gain comes not from choosing the "perfect" software, but from moving off spreadsheets and manual processes altogether.
