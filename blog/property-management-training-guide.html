Property Management Training: Certifications, Courses & Career Guide 2026

March 8, 2026 · 13 min read · By PropertyCEO

Whether you're breaking into property management or you've been managing properties for years and want to level up, the right training and certifications can dramatically increase your earning potential and career trajectory. Certified property managers earn 15-30% more than their non-certified counterparts, and certifications are increasingly required by larger management companies and property owners.

This guide covers every major certification, the best training programs, what skills you actually need, and realistic salary expectations at every career stage.

Do You Need a License to Be a Property Manager?

It depends on your state. Most states require some form of real estate license to manage properties for others. Here's the breakdown:

  • Full real estate license required: California, New York, Texas, Florida, and most other states require a real estate broker's or salesperson's license to manage properties for compensation.
  • Property management-specific license: A few states (like Montana and Oregon) offer PM-specific licenses that are separate from general real estate licenses.
  • No license required: A handful of states (like Massachusetts and Maryland for certain activities) have exemptions for property managers, particularly those managing their own properties.
  • Managing your own property: Almost no state requires a license to manage properties you personally own.

⚠️ Check your state's real estate commission website for specific requirements. Managing without a required license can result in fines and legal liability. When in doubt, get licensed — it only takes a few weeks and costs $300-$1,000.

CPM — The Gold Standard

The Certified Property Manager (CPM) designation from IREM (Institute of Real Estate Management) is the most prestigious certification in property management. It's the equivalent of a CPA in accounting or a PMP in project management.

What it takes:

  • 3+ years of qualifying real estate management experience
  • Complete 7 IREM courses (or have qualifying education)
  • Submit a Management Plan for a real property
  • Pass the CPM Certification Exam
  • Maintain through annual continuing education

ROI: CPM holders report average compensation 25-35% higher than non-certified peers. Many PM company RFPs and property owner contracts specifically require or prefer CPM-designated managers.

RMP — Best for Residential Managers

The Residential Management Professional (RMP) from NARPM is designed specifically for residential property managers (single-family, small multi-family). It's more accessible than the CPM and directly relevant if you're managing houses and small apartment buildings.

Requirements:

  • Currently managing 100+ residential units
  • 2+ years in residential property management
  • Complete NARPM educational requirements
  • Abide by NARPM Code of Ethics
  • Pass the RMP designation exam

CAM — Best for Apartment Managers

The Certified Apartment Manager (CAM) is the industry standard for apartment community managers. If you manage or want to manage larger apartment communities (50+ units), this is your certification.

The CAM program covers:

  • Property maintenance and risk management
  • Marketing and leasing strategies
  • Financial management and budgeting
  • Human resources for on-site teams
  • Resident relations and retention
  • Legal compliance (Fair Housing, ADA, etc.)

Best Online Property Management Courses

Beyond formal certifications, there are excellent courses for both beginners and experienced managers looking to sharpen specific skills:

For Beginners

  • IREM Skills On-Demand: Self-paced courses on leasing, maintenance, budgeting. ~$200-400 each.
  • Udemy Property Management courses: Budget-friendly ($15-$50), good for fundamentals. Quality varies — check ratings.
  • State-specific pre-licensing courses: Required before taking your real estate license exam. Usually $200-$500.
  • NAA Education Institute: Online courses specifically for apartment management. Great structure and curriculum.

For Experienced Managers

  • IREM Courses (CPM track): Deep dives into finance, maintenance, leadership, marketing. University-level quality.
  • NARPM Designation Courses: Focused on residential management best practices.
  • CCIM Institute: For managers moving into commercial real estate investment analysis.
  • Grace Hill/Visto: Online compliance training (Fair Housing, safety, maintenance). Used by major PM companies.

Essential Skills for Property Managers

No certification replaces real skills. Here are the capabilities that separate great property managers from average ones:

Financial Management

  • Budgeting and forecasting: Creating and managing operating budgets, capital improvement budgets, and cash flow projections
  • Financial reporting: Understanding P&L statements, balance sheets, and owner reports
  • Rent pricing: Setting competitive rents using market analysis and ROI calculations
  • Bookkeeping: Tracking income, expenses, security deposits, and trust accounts

Legal Knowledge

  • Fair Housing Act: Understanding protected classes and avoiding discrimination
  • Lease agreements: Drafting, enforcing, and interpreting rental contracts
  • Eviction process: Following proper legal procedures for each state
  • Security deposit laws: Handling deposits according to state regulations
  • ADA compliance: Reasonable accommodations and accessibility requirements

Marketing & Leasing

  • Vacancy reduction: Effective advertising, showing techniques, and rapid lease-up strategies
  • Tenant screening: Background checks, income verification, reference checks
  • Retention: Keeping good tenants is cheaper than finding new ones — develop resident satisfaction programs

Maintenance & Operations

  • Preventive maintenance programs: Scheduled inspections and upkeep to prevent costly emergency repairs
  • Vendor management: Building relationships with reliable contractors, negotiating pricing, managing quality
  • Emergency response: Having plans for floods, fires, HVAC failures, and other emergencies

People Skills

  • Conflict resolution: Mediating tenant disputes, neighbor complaints, and owner disagreements
  • Communication: Clear, professional communication with owners, tenants, contractors, and team members
  • Team leadership: Managing maintenance staff, leasing agents, and assistant managers

Career Path: From Entry Level to PM Company Owner

  1. Year 0-1: Leasing Agent or Assistant PM — Learn the basics. Show properties, process applications, handle maintenance requests. Get your real estate license.
  2. Year 1-3: Property Manager — Manage a property or portfolio directly. Learn budgeting, vendor management, owner relations. Start working toward CAM or RMP.
  3. Year 3-5: Senior PM or Portfolio Manager — Oversee multiple properties or a large community. Develop leadership skills. Earn your CPM.
  4. Year 5-10: Regional Manager or Director — Strategic oversight of multiple communities/portfolios. Focus on financial optimization and team development.
  5. Year 5+: Start Your Own PM Company — Use your experience and network to launch your own firm. Start with 20-50 doors you manage for other investors. Scale from there.

Continuing Education Requirements

Property management is a regulated industry, and most licenses and certifications require ongoing education:

  • Real estate license: Most states require 12-45 hours of continuing education every 1-2 years
  • CPM: Minimum of 10 contact hours per year through IREM programs
  • RMP/MPM: Annual education requirements through NARPM
  • CAM/CAPS: Renewal education through NAA
  • Fair Housing: Annual refresher training recommended (some states require it)

Pro tip: Most continuing education requirements can be fulfilled through online courses, industry conferences, and webinars. Many PM software companies and industry associations offer free or low-cost CE credits.

Building Your Training Plan

Here's a practical roadmap based on where you are:

Just Starting Out

  1. Get your state real estate license (4-8 weeks)
  2. Take a Fair Housing course (1 day)
  3. Get hired as a leasing agent or assistant PM
  4. Start ARM coursework within 6 months

1-3 Years Experience

  1. Earn your CAM or start RMP requirements
  2. Take financial management courses (budgeting, reporting)
  3. Develop technology skills (PM software, automation)
  4. Attend NARPM or NAA conference

3+ Years — Going for CPM

  1. Verify you meet CPM experience requirements
  2. Plan your IREM coursework (7 courses, can be done in 12-18 months)
  3. Start building your Management Plan portfolio
  4. Network with current CPM holders for mentorship
