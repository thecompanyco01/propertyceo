Best CRM for Property Management Companies in 2026

March 6, 2026 · 15 min read · By PropertyCEO

A CRM (Customer Relationship Management) system is one of the most underused tools in property management. Most PMs track owner leads in spreadsheets, sticky notes, or — worst of all — their heads. Then they wonder why they're closing only 15% of leads when they should be closing 30%+.

The right CRM transforms your sales process. It ensures no lead gets forgotten, every follow-up happens on time, and your pipeline is visible at a glance. Here's our honest comparison of the best options for property management companies in 2026.

Why Property Managers Need a CRM

A CRM isn't just a contact database — it's a sales engine. Here's what it does for your property management company:

  • Tracks every owner lead from first contact to signed agreement (or lost deal)
  • Automates follow-up reminders so you never forget to call a warm lead
  • Shows your pipeline — how many leads at each stage, expected revenue, conversion rates
  • Stores all communications — emails, call notes, meeting notes in one place
  • Identifies bottlenecks — where are you losing leads? At first call? After the proposal? Knowing this is gold.

Real talk: If you're managing fewer than 20 doors and generating fewer than 5 leads per month, a spreadsheet might be fine for now. But the moment you're handling 10+ active leads, you need a real CRM. The deals you'll lose to poor follow-up will cost more than any CRM subscription.

LeadSimple — Best for Property Management Companies

Price: $65/month (base) + per-user fees | Free trial: 14 days | Best for: PM companies with 30+ doors

LeadSimple was built specifically for property management companies. It understands your workflow: owner leads, management agreements, onboarding, and ongoing owner relations. It's not a generic CRM adapted for PM — it's PM-native.

Key Features

  • PM-specific pipeline stages (lead → appointment → proposal → agreement → onboarding)
  • Automated follow-up sequences via email and text
  • Integration with AppFolio, Buildium, and Rent Manager
  • Process automation for owner onboarding
  • Call tracking with recording
  • Team performance dashboards

Pros

  • Built specifically for property management — no customization needed
  • Excellent automations that save hours per week
  • Strong PM software integrations
  • Great training resources and support

Cons

  • Higher price point than generic CRMs
  • Learning curve for automation features
  • Overkill if you're under 20 doors

Our verdict: If you're a property management company with 30+ doors and serious about growth, LeadSimple is the best choice. The PM-specific features save you from hours of customization. Worth every penny.

HubSpot — Best Free Option

Price: Free (basic) / $20-800/month (paid tiers) | Best for: Startups and small PMs

HubSpot's free CRM is genuinely useful — not a crippled demo. You get contact management, deal tracking, email templates, and basic reporting for free. It's the best option if you're just starting out and can't justify $65+/month for a CRM.

Key Features (Free Tier)

  • Up to 1,000,000 contacts (seriously)
  • Deal pipeline with drag-and-drop stages
  • Email templates and tracking
  • Meeting scheduler
  • Basic reporting dashboard
  • Mobile app

Pros

  • Free tier is genuinely useful
  • Intuitive interface — easy to learn
  • Scales with you (paid tiers add features as you grow)
  • Huge ecosystem of integrations

Cons

  • Not PM-specific — requires customization for property management workflows
  • Paid tiers get expensive fast ($800/month for Professional)
  • No built-in PM software integrations

Our verdict: Start with HubSpot free when you're at 0-30 doors. Customize the pipeline stages for your PM workflow. Switch to LeadSimple when you're ready to invest in a PM-specific tool.

Follow Up Boss — Best for High-Volume Lead Sources

Price: $58/month per user | Free trial: 14 days | Best for: PMs spending heavily on Google Ads

Follow Up Boss is popular in real estate and works well for property managers who generate a lot of leads through paid advertising. Its speed-to-lead features ensure you respond to new inquiries within minutes.

Key Features

  • Automatic lead routing and assignment
  • Speed-to-lead alerts (respond in under 5 minutes)
  • Built-in calling and texting
  • Smart lists for lead prioritization
  • Integrations with 200+ lead sources

Pros

  • Excellent at capturing and distributing leads quickly
  • Built-in communication tools (call, text, email from one platform)
  • Great mobile app for on-the-go management

Cons

  • Per-user pricing gets expensive with a team
  • Built for real estate agents — needs customization for PM
  • No PM software integrations

Our verdict: Great if you're generating 20+ leads/month through advertising and need fast response times. Less ideal for smaller operations where a simpler CRM would do.

Pipedrive — Best for Simplicity

Price: $14-99/month per user | Free trial: 14 days | Best for: Solo PMs who want simple deal tracking

Pipedrive is a visual, pipeline-focused CRM. It's dead simple to use — if you can drag and drop, you can use Pipedrive. No learning curve, no feature bloat.

Key Features

  • Visual drag-and-drop pipeline
  • Activity reminders and follow-up scheduling
  • Email integration
  • Customizable deal stages
  • Reporting and insights

Pros

  • Incredibly easy to learn and use
  • Affordable starting price ($14/month)
  • Clean, visual interface
  • Good mobile app

Cons

  • Basic automation (compared to LeadSimple or HubSpot)
  • No PM-specific features
  • Limited free tier

Our verdict: If you want something better than a spreadsheet but simpler than LeadSimple, Pipedrive is a solid middle ground. Best for solo operators managing the sales process themselves.

AppFolio CRM — Best All-in-One

Price: Included with AppFolio ($280/month minimum) | Best for: AppFolio users wanting CRM integration

AppFolio has added CRM-like features directly into their property management platform. If you're already on AppFolio, using their built-in owner lead tracking means one less system to manage.

Key Features

  • Owner lead tracking within AppFolio
  • Pipeline management
  • Basic email templates
  • Owner portal with self-service features
  • Seamless transition from lead to active owner

Pros

  • No additional cost if you're already on AppFolio
  • Seamless data flow from lead to managed property
  • One system for everything

Cons

  • CRM features are basic compared to dedicated tools
  • Only makes sense if you're already paying for AppFolio
  • Limited automation compared to LeadSimple

Our verdict: If you're on AppFolio and generating fewer than 10 leads/month, the built-in CRM may be sufficient. For serious growth, pair it with LeadSimple for the best of both worlds.

How to Choose the Right CRM

Use this decision framework:

  • 0-20 doors, tight budget: HubSpot Free or Pipedrive ($14/month)
  • 20-50 doors, growing steadily: LeadSimple or HubSpot Starter
  • 50+ doors, team of 2+: LeadSimple (the PM-specific features justify the cost)
  • High-volume ads (20+ leads/month): Follow Up Boss for speed-to-lead
  • Already on AppFolio: Try AppFolio's built-in CRM first, add LeadSimple if you outgrow it

Setting Up Your CRM for Success

Buying a CRM is easy. Getting value from it requires setup. Here's how to make sure yours actually works:

1. Define Your Pipeline Stages

Standard property management pipeline:

  1. New Lead (just inquired)
  2. Contacted (had first conversation)
  3. Meeting Scheduled (property evaluation or call booked)
  4. Proposal Sent (management agreement sent for review)
  5. Negotiating (discussing terms)
  6. Won (signed agreement!)
  7. Lost (didn't close — track the reason)

2. Set Up Automated Follow-Ups

At minimum, create automated reminders for:

  • Follow up 24 hours after first contact
  • Follow up 3 days after sending proposal
  • Monthly check-in for leads in "Not Ready" status

3. Track the Right Metrics

  • Total leads generated per month
  • Conversion rate by pipeline stage
  • Average time from lead to close
  • Cost per lead (by source)
  • Revenue per lead source
