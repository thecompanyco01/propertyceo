What Is Buildium?

Buildium is a cloud-based property management platform built for professional property managers and management companies. It covers the full lifecycle — marketing vacant units, screening tenants, managing leases, collecting rent, handling maintenance, running accounting, and reporting to property owners.

Unlike free tools such as TurboTenant or Avail, Buildium is designed for teams running a property management business, not individual landlords managing their own units. It includes owner portals, trust accounting, staff management, and professional-grade financial reporting.

Buildium competes primarily with AppFolio and Rent Manager, but positions itself as more accessible and affordable than both. For our detailed head-to-head, see Buildium vs AppFolio.

Buildium Features: What Actually Works

Accounting & Financial Reporting (⭐ Excellent)

Buildium's accounting is the primary reason PM companies choose it. You get a full general ledger, trust accounting (critical for PM companies managing owner funds), bank reconciliation, accounts payable, and comprehensive financial reporting. Owner statements generate automatically, and 1099s are handled at year-end.

For PM companies graduating from QuickBooks, this is transformative. No more exporting, importing, and reconciling between systems. Everything — rent payments, maintenance costs, management fees, owner distributions — lives in one ledger. The accounting alone justifies the subscription for most companies.

Leasing & Marketing (⭐ Very Good)

Buildium syndicates listings to major rental sites, manages the application process, runs tenant screening through TransUnion, and handles e-lease signing. The prospect-to-tenant workflow is smooth — applications, screening, approval, lease signing, and move-in all flow through a single process.

The Growth plan adds tenant application grouping (important for multi-roommate situations) and rental history verification. The listing syndication isn't as broad as TurboTenant's 20+ sites, but it covers the major platforms.

Maintenance Management (⭐ Very Good)

Tenants submit requests through the resident portal, and Buildium routes them based on your workflows. Work orders track the full lifecycle with vendor assignment, cost tracking, and communication logs. The Growth and Premium plans add project management, templates, and maintenance billing.

The Premium plan includes property inspections — a valuable feature for PM companies that need to document property condition regularly. The maintenance contact center (Premium only) provides after-hours emergency support, which is a genuine differentiator for companies that struggle with 2 AM emergency calls.

Owner Portal & Reporting (⭐ Very Good)

Property owners get their own login to view financial statements, maintenance updates, and property performance. This is table stakes for professional PM companies — owners expect transparency, and Buildium delivers it. Reports are professional-looking and customizable (especially on Growth and Premium plans).

Resident Portal & Online Payments (⭐ Good)

Tenants pay rent online via ACH or credit card, set up autopay, submit maintenance requests, and communicate with management through the portal. The experience is functional but not exceptional — some tenants find the portal dated compared to consumer apps they're used to.

Buildium also offers payment alternatives like Obligo (deposit-free renting), Livble, and retail cash payments for unbanked tenants. These options set it apart from simpler platforms.

Lumina AI (⭐ Developing)

Buildium's AI features, branded as "Lumina," are still evolving. The AI assistant helps with in-app guidance, "Write with Lumina" drafts communications, and the Premium plan adds AI bill scanning and workflow automations. These features are useful but not yet at the level of AppFolio's AI leasing assistant. Give it time — Buildium is clearly investing here.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does Buildium really cost?

Buildium's base plans start at $62/month (Essential), $192/month (Growth), and $400/month (Premium). However, many essential features like ePay, tenant screening, and eSignatures carry additional per-transaction fees. A realistic budget for a 150-unit company on the Growth plan is $250-$400/month total. For detailed pricing, see our Buildium pricing guide.

Does Buildium offer a free trial?

Buildium occasionally offers a 14-day free trial. Check their website for current availability. They also provide guided demos. We recommend starting with the demo to understand the platform before committing, as the onboarding process takes time and effort.

Is Buildium good for small landlords?

Not really. Buildium is designed for professional property management companies, not individual landlords. If you self-manage fewer than 20 units, the cost and complexity aren't justified. Use TurboTenant (free), Avail, or Hemlane instead.

Can Buildium handle HOA management?

Yes — this is actually one of Buildium's strengths. It supports community association management with features like violation tracking, board member portals, amenity booking, and community calendars. If you manage both rental properties and HOAs, Buildium is one of the few platforms that handles both well.

Is Buildium better than AppFolio?

It depends on your size and needs. Buildium is better for 50-200 units (more affordable, easier to learn). AppFolio is better for 200+ units (stronger AI, better commercial support). See our complete Buildium vs AppFolio comparison for the full breakdown.
