Best Property Management Software for Small Landlords (2026)

You don't need enterprise software to manage a few rental properties. You need something simple, affordable (ideally free), and effective enough to handle tenants, leases, rent, and maintenance without eating your evenings. Quick answer: Most small landlords should start with TurboTenant (free) or Innago (free). If you need accounting, add Stessa (free). Only pay for software once you pass 20-30 units or start managing for other owners.

1. TurboTenant — Best Free Option for Beginners

⭐ 4.2/5 💰 Free

Best for: First-time landlords with 1-10 units who want the simplest possible setup.

TurboTenant is the easiest property management software to get started with. It's free for landlords, requires no technical setup, and its listing syndication to 20+ sites (Zillow, Apartments.com, Realtor.com) is unmatched among free tools.

Key features: Listing syndication (20+ sites), tenant screening (TransUnion), online lease signing, ACH rent collection, maintenance requests.

Limitations: No real accounting. Basic maintenance tracking. No owner portal.

Who should skip it: Landlords who need robust accounting (pair with Stessa) or property management companies (use Buildium).

2. Innago — Most Complete Free PM Software

⭐ 4.1/5 💰 Free

Best for: Landlords with 5-25 units who want comprehensive PM features without paying.

Innago offers more features for free than any other PM platform. Online leases with e-signatures, free ACH for both parties, tenant screening, maintenance tracking, expense reporting, and tenant communication — all at zero cost. It's the tool to graduate to when TurboTenant feels too basic.

Key features: Custom lease builder, free ACH (both parties), tenant screening, maintenance tracking, expense tracking, late fee automation.

Limitations: No mobile app. Basic listing syndication. UI less polished than competitors.

Who should skip it: Landlords who prioritize listing reach (use TurboTenant) or want a mobile app (use Avail).

3. Stessa — Best Free Accounting for Landlords

⭐ 4.3/5 💰 Free

Best for: DIY landlords who need automated financial tracking and tax-ready reports.

Stessa isn't property management software — it's a specialized accounting tool for rental properties. Connect your bank accounts, and it automatically tracks income/expenses, categorizes transactions, and generates IRS Schedule E reports. It's the best free accounting tool in the rental space, period.

Key features: Automated bank account syncing, transaction categorization, Schedule E reports, cash flow dashboard, receipt scanning, portfolio performance tracking.

Limitations: Not a PM tool — no screening, leases, or maintenance. Basic rent collection. Owned by Roofstock.

Pro tip: Pair Stessa with TurboTenant or Innago for a complete, free stack. TurboTenant handles PM; Stessa handles money.

4. Avail (by Realtor.com) — Best Tenant-Facing Features

⭐ 4.0/5 💰 Free / $7 per unit/mo

Best for: Landlords with 1-10 units who want polished, tenant-friendly tools.

Avail (now part of Realtor.com) offers a clean, modern interface with strong tenant-facing features. Tenants get a nice portal for applications, payments, and maintenance requests. The free plan is solid; the Unlimited plan ($7/unit/mo) removes ACH fees, unlocks custom leases, and adds premium features.

Key features: Listing syndication (10+ sites), tenant screening, online leases, rent collection, maintenance tracking, mobile-friendly design.

Limitations: Free plan charges tenants $2.50/ACH. Premium gets expensive at scale ($7/unit). No full accounting.

Who should skip it: Landlords who want free ACH (use Innago) or need accounting (add Stessa).

5. DoorLoop — Best Paid Software for Growing Landlords

⭐ 4.0/5 💰 $59/mo

Best for: Landlords growing from 20 to 200 units who want modern, full-featured software.

DoorLoop is the best mid-range option for landlords who've outgrown free tools but aren't ready for Buildium or AppFolio pricing. It has the most modern UI in the category, built-in accounting, unlimited units on all plans, and handles residential + commercial + HOA.

Key features: Modern UI, full accounting, tenant/owner portals (Pro+), maintenance management, listing syndication, e-signatures, rent collection.

Limitations: Starter plan too limited. No AI features. Younger company. Pro plan ($109/mo) needed for full value.

Who should skip it: Landlords with under 15 units (free tools are better value) or large PMs with 500+ units (use AppFolio).

6. Buildium — Best for Professional Property Managers

⭐ 4.2/5 💰 $58/mo

Best for: Professional PMs managing 50-500 units for property owners.

Buildium is the industry standard for professional property management companies. It's not the cheapest or the flashiest, but it's battle-tested, reliable, and covers every PM workflow you need: accounting, leasing, maintenance, owner reporting, tenant portals, and compliance.

Key features: Full trust accounting, owner portal + reporting, tenant screening, online leasing, maintenance workflows, 1099 e-filing, free PM website.

Limitations: Interface feels dated. No AI features. Some features locked behind higher tiers ($183-$375/mo).

Who should skip it: Self-managing landlords with under 30 units (overkill + expensive per door). Landlords who don't manage for others.

7. RentRedi — Best Mobile-First Experience

⭐ 3.9/5 💰 $12/mo (annual) | $20/mo (monthly)

Best for: Landlords with 1-20 units who manage everything from their phone.

RentRedi is built mobile-first. The app is genuinely good — not a scaled-down version of the web platform, but designed from the ground up for phone-based property management. Tenant screening, rent collection, maintenance tracking, and lease management all work smoothly from your pocket.

Key features: Excellent mobile app, tenant screening, rent collection (ACH + card), maintenance tracking with video requests, prequalification pages, listing syndication.

Limitations: Not free (though $12/mo is cheap). Web interface is less polished than mobile. No full accounting. Limited reporting.

Who should skip it: Landlords who prefer desktop workflows, anyone who needs robust accounting or owner portals.

8. Hemlane — Best for Remote/Hybrid Management

⭐ 3.8/5 💰 $30/mo (Basic) | $60/mo (Essential)

Best for: Remote landlords who want a blend of software and human assistance for local tasks.

Hemlane is unique — it offers a hybrid model where you manage your properties through software, but can tap into Hemlane's agent network for local tasks like showings, inspections, and maintenance coordination. It's ideal for out-of-state landlords who don't want a full property manager but need some boots on the ground.

Key features: 24/7 maintenance repair coordination, local agent network, online rent collection, tenant screening, listing syndication, owner dashboard.

Limitations: More expensive than other small landlord tools. Agent network quality varies by market. Not a full PM replacement.

Who should skip it: Local landlords who can handle things in person, landlords with budgets under $30/mo for software.

Do Small Landlords Actually Need PM Software?

If you own just one or two rentals, you might wonder if you even need software. Here's our honest take:

  • 1-2 units: You can get by with spreadsheets and Venmo, but free software like TurboTenant makes your life noticeably easier and costs nothing
  • 3-5 units: You should definitely be using PM software. The time savings on rent collection and tenant screening alone justify it
  • 5-10 units: PM software is essential. Without it, you're leaving money on the table through inefficiency and missed late fees
  • 10+ units: This is non-negotiable. You need software, and you should consider whether it's time for a more robust platform

The best part? With tools like TurboTenant, Innago, and Stessa all being free, there's literally no financial reason not to use PM software. Zero cost, significant time savings.

What Features Actually Matter for Small Landlords?

Enterprise PM software has hundreds of features. As a small landlord, here's what you actually use daily:

  1. Rent collection (must-have) — Automating this eliminates late payments and awkward conversations
  2. Tenant screening (must-have) — Bad tenants cost 10x more than screening fees
  3. Listing syndication (very important) — Reduces vacancy time, which is your biggest expense
  4. Lease management (important) — E-signatures save time and create better records
  5. Expense tracking (important) — Especially at tax time. Even basic tracking beats spreadsheets
  6. Maintenance requests (nice-to-have) — Useful for documentation, not critical at 5 units
  7. Owner portal (only if managing for others) — Essential for PMs, irrelevant for self-managers

Frequently Asked Questions

What's the best free property management software?

TurboTenant for simplicity and listing reach. Innago for the most complete free feature set. Stessa for financial tracking and accounting. All three are genuinely free with no unit limits.

When should I switch from free to paid software?

When you hit 20-30 units, when you start managing properties for other owners, or when you need professional-grade accounting and reporting. Until then, free tools handle everything a small landlord needs.

Can I use multiple tools together?

Absolutely — and we recommend it. The best free stack is TurboTenant (PM) + Stessa (accounting). Together they cover 95% of small landlord needs at $0/month.

Is Buildium overkill for a small landlord?

If you self-manage under 30 units, yes. Buildium is designed for professional PM companies with owner clients. At $58/mo, you're paying $2-6/door for features you probably don't need yet. Start free, upgrade when you outgrow it.

What about Rent Manager?

Rent Manager is excellent but designed for large operations (500+ units). It's extremely customizable but complex to set up. Small landlords should not consider it — it's like using a bulldozer to plant a garden.
