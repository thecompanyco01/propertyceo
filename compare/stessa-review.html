What Is Stessa?

Stessa is a financial tracking platform built specifically for rental property investors. Think of it as Mint or Personal Capital, but designed for landlords. You connect your bank accounts, and Stessa automatically pulls in transactions, categorizes them by property, and generates financial reports.

The platform was acquired by Roofstock (the online marketplace for buying/selling investment properties) in 2022. This matters because:

  • Stessa is financially stable — it's backed by a well-funded parent company
  • There's potential for deeper integration with Roofstock's marketplace
  • Some users worry about data being used to market Roofstock services (reasonable concern, but no evidence of abuse so far)

Stessa Features: What Actually Works

Automated Financial Tracking (⭐ Excellent)

Connect your bank accounts and credit cards via Plaid, and Stessa automatically imports transactions and categorizes them. Mortgage payments, insurance, property taxes, repairs — it recognizes common rental expenses and assigns them to the right property.

The categorization isn't perfect (about 85% accuracy in our testing), but it's easy to review and correct the remaining 15%. Once you correct a categorization, Stessa learns and applies it to future transactions. Over time, accuracy improves significantly.

This is genuinely game-changing for landlords who've been using spreadsheets. Instead of manually entering every expense, you review a categorized feed and approve or adjust. It cuts bookkeeping time by 80%+.

Tax-Ready Reports (⭐ Excellent)

Stessa generates IRS Schedule E reports that you can hand directly to your CPA or import into tax software. Income and expenses are pre-categorized into the exact line items the IRS wants. This alone is worth using Stessa — at tax time, you'll save hours.

Additional reports include: net cash flow, capital expenses, depreciation schedules (basic), and property performance summaries. All exportable to PDF or CSV.

Dashboard & Portfolio View (⭐ Very Good)

The dashboard gives you at-a-glance metrics: total income, expenses, net cash flow, occupancy, and portfolio value. You can drill down into individual properties or view your entire portfolio. It's clean, fast, and genuinely useful.

The property-level view shows monthly income vs expenses, YTD performance, and allows you to upload documents (leases, insurance, inspections). It's not as detailed as purpose-built PM software, but for financial tracking it's excellent.

Rent Collection (⭐ Basic)

Stessa added rent collection in recent years. Tenants can pay via ACH, and payments are automatically tracked in your financial dashboard. It works, but it's bare-bones compared to TurboTenant or Avail:

  • No autopay scheduling for tenants
  • No late fee automation
  • No credit card payment option
  • No tenant portal or communication tools

It's fine if you just want rent to show up automatically in your accounting. It's not a replacement for a proper rent collection platform.

Document Storage (⭐ Good)

Upload and organize documents by property: leases, insurance policies, inspection reports, receipts. The free plan has storage limits; Pro gives you unlimited storage. It's a nice organizational feature but nothing special — you could do the same with Google Drive folders.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Stessa really free?

Yes. The Essential plan is free with no unit limits, no trial period, and no credit card required. Stessa makes money through the Pro subscription ($20/mo) and through partnerships (Roofstock marketplace referrals, insurance partners, etc.). The free plan is their growth engine — they keep it genuinely useful.

What happened when Roofstock bought Stessa?

Roofstock acquired Stessa in 2022. Since then, the product has continued to improve with regular updates. There's been no paywall added to previously free features. The main concern is that Roofstock might eventually use Stessa data to market properties to you — so far, it's been respectful (opt-in only), but it's worth being aware of.

Can Stessa replace QuickBooks for rental properties?

For simple rental portfolios (single-family, small multi-family), yes. Stessa's automated categorization and Schedule E reports handle most of what landlords use QuickBooks for. For complex situations (LLCs, multiple entities, commercial properties, 1031 exchanges), you'll still want QuickBooks or a dedicated CPA.

Does Stessa integrate with property management software?

Not directly. There's no native integration with Buildium, AppFolio, DoorLoop, or other PM tools. You can export data from Stessa to CSV and import it elsewhere, but there's no automated sync. This is one of Stessa's biggest limitations.

How accurate is Stessa's auto-categorization?

In our testing, about 85% of transactions were correctly categorized automatically. The remaining 15% required manual review. Accuracy improves over time as Stessa learns from your corrections. Plan to spend 15-30 minutes per month reviewing and correcting categories — still a massive time savings over manual bookkeeping.
