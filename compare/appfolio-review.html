What Is AppFolio?

AppFolio Property Manager is a cloud-based property management platform designed for professional management companies handling residential, commercial, student housing, and community association portfolios. Unlike free tools like TurboTenant or Avail, AppFolio is built for teams — multiple staff members, owner reporting, trust accounting, and enterprise-grade workflows.

The platform covers the entire property management lifecycle: marketing and leasing, tenant screening, rent collection, accounting, maintenance management, owner reporting, and more. AppFolio has also invested heavily in AI features, including an AI leasing assistant, smart maintenance coordination, and predictive analytics.

AppFolio is a publicly traded company (NASDAQ: APPF) with over 19,000 property management customers. They're well-funded, actively developing their product, and not going anywhere — which matters when you're entrusting your entire business to a platform.

The real cost is higher than the per-unit price suggests. AppFolio charges additional fees for many services:

  • Tenant screening: $15-$30 per applicant
  • ACH payments: Free for tenants, but credit card payments carry a processing fee
  • eCheck processing: Included in subscription
  • Onboarding: Included, but expect 4-8 weeks for full implementation
  • Data migration: Included for standard migrations from major competitors

For a company managing 500 units on the Core plan, expect to pay around $745/month ($1.49 × 500). On Plus, that jumps to $1,600/month. These are real costs — but for a company managing $5M+ in annual rent, AppFolio typically pays for itself through efficiency gains and reduced staffing needs.

AppFolio Features: What Actually Works

Accounting & Financial Reporting (⭐ Excellent)

This is AppFolio's crown jewel. The platform offers full general ledger accounting with trust accounting, bank reconciliation, accounts payable/receivable, and CAM reconciliation. You get owner statements, P&L reports, balance sheets, cash flow statements, and 1099 generation — all built into the platform.

For PM companies coming from QuickBooks, this is a game-changer. No more exporting data between systems, no more reconciliation headaches. Everything lives in one place. The accounting is GAAP-compliant and audit-ready, which matters for larger portfolios with institutional owners.

AI Leasing Assistant (⭐ Impressive)

AppFolio's AI leasing assistant, Lisa, responds to prospect inquiries 24/7, answers common questions about listings, pre-qualifies leads, and schedules tours — all without human intervention. It integrates with your listings on Zillow, Apartments.com, and your website.

In our testing, Lisa handled roughly 60-70% of initial prospect interactions without needing a human handoff. For a PM company receiving hundreds of inquiries per month, this saves significant leasing staff time. It's one of the best AI features we've seen in any PM software.

Maintenance Management (⭐ Very Good)

Tenants submit requests through their portal, and AppFolio routes them based on your rules — by property, maintenance type, priority level, and vendor availability. Work orders track the full lifecycle from request to completion, with vendor communication, photo documentation, and cost tracking.

The Smart Maintenance feature (Plus and Max plans) uses AI to triage requests, suggest troubleshooting steps to tenants, and automatically route urgent issues. It's not perfect — complex maintenance scenarios still need human judgment — but it reduces the volume of calls your team handles daily.

Online Payments & Rent Collection (⭐ Very Good)

Tenants pay via ACH, credit card, or debit card through their resident portal. Autopay is easy to set up, late fees are automated, and payment tracking is clean. AppFolio deposits funds quickly — typically 1-2 business days for ACH.

The platform also handles owner disbursements automatically. Once rent is collected and expenses are accounted for, owner payments can be set to auto-distribute. This alone saves PM companies hours of work each month.

Owner Portal & Reporting (⭐ Excellent)

This is where AppFolio separates from tools like Buildium for larger operations. Property owners get their own portal with real-time access to financial statements, maintenance updates, lease details, and property performance metrics. You can customize what owners see and how reports are formatted.

For PM companies, the owner portal is a retention tool. Happy owners who can see exactly what's happening with their properties are less likely to switch management companies. AppFolio understands this and has invested heavily in the owner experience.

Marketing & Leasing (⭐ Good)

AppFolio syndicates listings to major sites and offers built-in property websites. The application process is smooth — online applications, tenant screening integration, and e-lease signing all flow together. However, the listing syndication isn't as broad as TurboTenant's free offering. You're paying more for the integrated workflow, not necessarily wider reach.

Mobile App (⭐ Good)

AppFolio's mobile app lets property managers and staff handle inspections, maintenance, leasing tasks, and communication on the go. It's functional but not exceptional — some users report it's slower than the web interface and occasionally buggy. It gets the job done for field work.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does AppFolio cost per month?

AppFolio starts at $1.49 per unit per month on the Core plan, with a minimum monthly fee of $298. The Plus plan is $3.20/unit/month (minimum $640), and the Max plan is $5.00/unit/month (minimum $1,000). Additional fees apply for tenant screening, payment processing, and certain add-on services. For exact pricing, you'll need to request a custom quote from AppFolio.

Does AppFolio have a free trial?

No. AppFolio does not offer a free trial. They provide demos and guided walkthroughs instead. This is one of our complaints — committing to a platform with a long onboarding process without being able to try it first is a significant ask. Request a thorough demo and ask for customer references before signing.

Is AppFolio good for small landlords?

No. AppFolio has a minimum portfolio size requirement (typically 200 units) and a minimum monthly fee that makes it impractical for small landlords. If you manage fewer than 50 units, look at TurboTenant (free), Avail, or Innago. For 50-200 units, Buildium is a better fit.

Can AppFolio handle commercial properties?

Yes. AppFolio supports residential, commercial, student housing, and community association (HOA/condo) portfolios. The commercial features include CAM reconciliation, custom lease structures, and commercial-specific reporting. It's one of the few platforms that handles mixed residential/commercial portfolios well.

How long does AppFolio onboarding take?

Expect 4-8 weeks for full onboarding, including data migration, staff training, and workflow configuration. Larger portfolios or complex migrations can take longer. AppFolio provides a dedicated onboarding specialist, but the process requires significant time commitment from your team.

What's better, AppFolio or Buildium?

It depends on your portfolio size. Buildium is better for 50-200 units — it's more affordable, easier to learn, and has most features PM companies need. AppFolio is better for 200+ units — it has stronger AI features, better commercial support, and more sophisticated accounting. See our detailed Buildium vs AppFolio comparison.
