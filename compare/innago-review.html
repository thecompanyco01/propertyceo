Innago is the most feature-rich free property management software available in 2026. Where TurboTenant excels at listing and screening but falls short on accounting, Innago tries to be a complete PM solution — for free. Online leases, rent collection, tenant screening, maintenance tracking, expense reporting, and more — all without a subscription fee.

The catch? There is no catch, actually. Innago makes money from tenant-facing services (renter's insurance, credit reporting, payment processing fees). The landlord-facing product is genuinely free. But "free" doesn't mean "perfect" — let's get into the details.

🏢 Company: Founded in 2017 in Cincinnati, OH. Bootstrapped and profitable.

💰 Pricing: Free for all landlords — no tiers, no unit limits, no premium plan

🎯 Target User: Independent landlords managing 5-25 units who want more than basic tools

⚡ Best Feature: Complete PM functionality (leases, screening, rent, maintenance, accounting) — all free

How Does Innago Make Money If It's Free?

This is the first question everyone asks, and it's a smart one. If you're not paying for the product, you might be the product. Here's how Innago actually generates revenue:

  • Renter's insurance partnerships: Innago offers optional renter's insurance through partner providers and earns a commission on policies sold
  • Tenant credit reporting: Tenants can opt to have their rent payments reported to credit bureaus for a fee — Innago takes a cut
  • Payment processing fees: Credit card payments incur standard processing fees (2.9% + $0.30). ACH is free for both parties
  • Application fees: Tenant screening costs $35-$55 per applicant (paid by the tenant)

This model is sustainable because it's volume-based. The more landlords use Innago, the more tenants flow through, and the more tenant-facing services get purchased. It's similar to how TurboTenant works, but Innago offers more features for free because they have more revenue streams.

Online Leases & E-Signatures (⭐ Very Good)

Innago's lease builder is one of its strongest features. You can create custom leases from templates, add clauses, set terms, and send for e-signature — all within the platform. Leases are state-compliant and legally binding. Compared to TurboTenant's lease feature, Innago offers more customization.

You can also upload your own existing lease documents and just use Innago for the e-signature functionality. This flexibility is a big plus for landlords who already have attorney-reviewed leases they want to keep using.

Rent Collection (⭐ Very Good)

ACH bank transfers are free for both landlords and tenants. Autopay is available, late fees are automated, and partial payments can be configured. The tenant portal is clean and easy for tenants to navigate. Credit card payments are available but incur the standard 2.9% + $0.30 processing fee.

Compared to Avail (which charges $2.50/ACH on the free plan), Innago's completely free ACH is a significant advantage. This alone might justify choosing Innago over Avail for landlords who prioritize free rent collection.

Tenant Screening (⭐ Good)

Innago partners with TransUnion for screening. Reports include credit scores, criminal background, eviction history, and income verification. The tenant pays $35-$55 per application. Reports are comprehensive and delivered quickly (usually within minutes).

One nice touch: Innago lets you set minimum screening criteria that applicants see before applying. This self-filters unqualified applicants and saves everyone time.

Maintenance Tracking (⭐ Good)

Tenants submit maintenance requests through the portal with descriptions and photos. Landlords can track status, assign priorities, and communicate with tenants about repairs. It's more functional than TurboTenant's maintenance feature but less sophisticated than Buildium's or DoorLoop's workflow tools.

Missing: vendor management, work order templates, cost tracking per maintenance item, and automated vendor dispatching. For landlords who handle most repairs themselves or through a small network of contractors, it's sufficient.

Financial Reporting (⭐ Decent)

Innago tracks income and expenses and generates basic financial reports. It's a step up from spreadsheets but nowhere near Stessa's automated accounting or Buildium's full accounting suite. You can track expenses by property and category, generate P&L statements, and export data.

For landlords who need serious financial tracking, we recommend pairing Innago with Stessa (also free). Innago handles PM tasks; Stessa handles accounting. Together they're a powerful zero-cost stack.

Listing Syndication (⭐ Basic)

Innago syndicates listings to some rental sites, but its reach is nowhere near TurboTenant's 20+ site network. If listing syndication is your top priority, TurboTenant wins this category handily.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Innago really completely free?

Yes. There is no paid tier for landlords. Every feature is free, with no unit limits. Innago makes money from tenant-facing services (application fees, renter's insurance commissions, credit card processing fees, credit reporting fees). Landlords pay nothing.

Is Innago safe to use?

Innago uses industry-standard encryption and partners with established providers (TransUnion for screening, Stripe for payments). The company has been operating since 2017 without data breaches. It's as safe as any other cloud-based PM tool.

Does Innago have a mobile app?

No. Innago is web-only, though the website is mobile-responsive and works on phone browsers. If having a dedicated mobile app is important to you, consider TurboTenant or Avail instead.

Can Innago handle commercial properties?

Innago is designed primarily for residential properties. While you could technically use it for small commercial leases, it lacks CAM reconciliation, commercial lease features, and other tools that commercial PMs need. For commercial, look at AppFolio or DoorLoop.

When should I upgrade from Innago to paid software?

Consider upgrading when: you pass 25-30 units and need more sophisticated tools, when you start managing properties for other owners (need owner portal), when you need robust accounting beyond basic tracking, or when a mobile app becomes essential for your workflow. At that point, DoorLoop ($59/mo) or Buildium ($58/mo) are natural next steps.
